Features of the current digitization ae.txt,
 cdsl digitization of Apte's English-Sanskrit dictionary

The digitization is a text file in the utf-8 encoding.

The digitizaion has three parts:
- ae.txt dictionary entries
- ae_header.txt  front matter (for reference)
- ae_footer.txt  appendix (for reference)

ae.txt represents a sequence of ENTRIES.
Each ENTRY consists of:
 - a meta-line
 - 1 or more text lines,
 - <LEND>   (end-of-entry marker) (text following <LEND> is ignored by displays

For example:
<L>3<pc>001<k1>abase<k2>abase
{@Abase@}¦,Ⓒ<lex>v. t.</lex>Ⓓ<s>aBiBU</s> 1 <ab>P</ab>, .....
Ⓝ{@[Abase]ment@},Ⓒ<lex>s.</lex>Ⓓ<s>apakarzaH, ..... </s>
<LEND>

There are 0 or more lines between successive entries;
these lines are ignored by the displays.

The meta-line structure contains a fixed sequence of fields;
  each field is of the form <fieldname>fieldvalue.
<L>L<pc>PC<k1>K1<k2>K2<h>H<e>E
The fields are:
   L Cologne numeric identifier for the entry
     Unique for each entry
     For successive entries E1 and E2, L1 is numerically less than L2 
  PC page reference to scanned image
  K1 the headword spelling - displays search on K1
  k2 May contain additional information; usu. same as K1. Not used in displays
   H The homonym identifier - optional. Not used at this time
   E The entry-type - optional. Not used at this time

The first line of an Entry uses ¦ at the end of citation form of entry
 X¦text  X usually is {@K1@}, but may have other forms

The entry text contains several forms of markup.
XML TAGS 
<ab>X</ab>   35746    Abbreviations
<i>X</i>      3028    X to display with italic text
<lex>X</lex> 22879    X is a part-of-speech
<ls>X</ls>    1141    X is a literary source 
<s>X</s>     72856    X is a Sanskrit word, in slp1 transliteration

{@X@}        33947    X to display as bold text
{{X->Y}}       162    a cdsl print change
{{Lbody=N}}      0    alternate headword entry (N is the L of another entry)
[PageN]       ~200    Indicates entry continues on Page N of scan images.
